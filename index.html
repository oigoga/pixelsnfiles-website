Meet your coach

Hi, I'm Oigoga.
I've been where you are.

Eight years ago I had big dreams of being an "independent woman." I just had no idea how to get there.

What I had going for me was the right circle and a lot of curiosity, so I started learning skills. And then I stayed in the learning phase for… a while. Take a course, try to do something with it, get stuck, stop, repeat. You know the cycle.

In 2019, on a bus to Ibadan, I heard about Fiverr and Upwork. People said they'd change my life, so I signed up to Fiverr that same day. I kept that account for ages and no matter what I did, it just wouldn't click.

Then 2021 happened. My friend started getting actual humans to actually pay her, and I was inspired. I picked up software development hoping to make it big in tech, and learned technical writing alongside it. I did a fellowship with Tech4Dev's Women Techsters, interned as a software developer at three different startups, and felt like I was finally moving.

Working at Paycrest, surrounded by genuinely fire developers, taught me two things. One, the well of tech was far deeper than I thought. Two, I wasn't ready to give everything it would take. So I pivoted.

I sat down and listed everything I was good at, and a surprising amount of my self-taught skills overlapped with management, admin and operations. On a call with Francis Ocholi, I decided this was the path, and that I'd stick with it until it paid off.

I picked Upwork and started sending proposals. I spent close to $400 on connects — roughly 3,000 of them — before I landed my first big-girl job. Then I became Top Rated, and Top Rated Plus, within months. All while studying engineering at Obafemi Awolowo University.

Last year knocked me down a bit. My biggest contract ended abruptly, and around the same time a different client left me a bad review — a genuine mistake. So I did what I always do. I sat with it and dug into what actually worked.

And I found it. There's a formula for going from no skill, to properly skilled, to landing clients.

So what's better than me making good money on Upwork? Me and a whole lot of other people doing the same. I've always loved it when people reached out to learn this, and I've always been happy to teach. But only a few ever applied what I showed them — usually because of information overload, or because the standard classroom structure just didn't fit them.

That's the gap PixelsnFiles exists to close.

Enough yapping. It's lovely to meet you. — Goga
